Interface

The interface in public view that I choose to analyze is the self-checkout machine.

A self-checkout machine is a machine that allows customers to check out in a retail store without having to go through a clerk, which can be used to replace traditional cashiers. Self-checkout gives consumers more choices, convenience and speed, and avoiding long queues is also a major reason why many consumers prefer self-checkout.

Specifically, I will use CVS self-checkout machines as an example.

Sketch & Description of Interface

Users scan their items one-by-one using the barcode reader, and at the same time, users can bag their items. Then, the user only needs to select his preferred payment method (debit card/credit card/cash, etc.) to pay, and everything is all set.

Generally speaking, a self-checkout machine contains the following components:

  • Touch screen monitor
  • Basket stand
  • Barcode scanner cum weighing scale
  • ATM PIN pad

Observations & Interview Summaries

Q1: Do you prefer the self-checkout machine or checkout by cashier? And Why?

  • User 1: Self-checkout machine. Because I can bag my items in the way I like.
  • User 2: Checkout by cashiers. I need to wait much longer when my items cannot be scanned or the machine doesn’t work. And when I have a lot of items, I have to bag everything by myself (I am a really lazy person). And the weight sensor sometimes drives me crazy.
  • User 3: Self-checkout machine. I’d rather bag everything and avoid any kind of interactions with people.

Q2: Is there any part of the self-checkout machine interface that you are unsatisfied with?

  • User 1: It is difficult to scan the barcode of some items. And the card reader sometimes can’t read my card.
  • User 2: The sensor is sometimes too sensitive, but sometimes it cannot detect anything. I need to keep clicking "Skip Bagging'', or even ask others for help. And when I have a lot of items, the basket stand would mess up, especially on rainy days.
  • User 3: Generally no. It’s a pretty simple machine. But sometimes the scanner doesn't work well.

Empathy Maps

Eric is a rushed student and his next class will begin in 5 minutes. But when he is in the grocery store, there is some problem with the weight sensor and cannot detect any item. The user has to go and communicate with the clerk. The user waited for a long time before the clerk came over slowly.

John is an impatient person who always wants to use his time efficiently. He's also a bit introverted and doesn't like unnecessary interactions with people. When he wanted to check out, there happened to be an automatic checkout machine available, but there were still five people in line at the cashier's side.

Storyboard

Eric put milk on the weighing scale, but it cannot be scanned and nothing shows up on the screen.

Eric pressed “Request Help” and waited for a clerk to come to help him.

The screen just says “Please wait. Help is on the way.”

The clerk fixed this issue and Eric just checked his milk. And then pressed “Fish and Pay”.

Eric put his card in the card reader and entered his PIN.

Everything is all set! Eric put this milk into the bag and grabbed the receipt.
